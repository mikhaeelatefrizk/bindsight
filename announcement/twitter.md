## Thread (5 tweets — for higher engagement)

### 1/5
> 🧬 New open-source tool: **bindsight** — the first pipeline that takes RNA-seq
> counts as input and outputs ranked de novo protein binder candidates with
> full provenance back to the patient cohort. Live demo (no install):
> https://bindsight.streamlit.app
> 🧵👇

### 2/5
> Two ecosystems in computational biology have been working in parallel:
>
> **Genomics** (DESeq2, Seurat, TCGA) stops at *"here are interesting genes."*
>
> **Protein design** (RFdiffusion, ProteinMPNN, BindCraft, Boltz-2) starts at
> *"given a target structure..."*
>
> bindsight is the bridge.

### 3/5
> Three things had to align in late 2025 for one person to build this:
>
> 1. SURFACE-Bind catalog (PNAS 2025) — pre-computed targetable sites for ~2,800
>    surface proteins
> 2. Boltz-2/BoltzGen released MIT for code AND weights
> 3. Free Colab GPUs got powerful enough for RFdiff+MPNN
>
> All true now. Not before.

### 4/5
> The pipeline rediscovers HER2 + EGFR — the textbook cancer immunotherapy
> targets — from a synthetic 10-gene cohort in 30 seconds on a CPU laptop. No
> internet, no GPU. Try it: https://bindsight.streamlit.app/ (click Demo)

### 5/5
> 175 tests passing across Linux/macOS/Windows × Python 3.11/3.12.
>
> MIT licensed. Permanent DOI: https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20121496
>
> Code: https://github.com/mikhaeelatefrizk/bindsight
>
> Built solo — open to collaborators on the v0.2 validation paper.
